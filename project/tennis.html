Tennis: The Game of Elegance

Discover the history, championships, and legends of tennis

Origins of Tennis

Tennis traces its roots back to 12th-century France, where it was played with bare hands. By the 16th century, rackets were introduced, and the game evolved into what is now known as "real tennis." The modern version of lawn tennis was standardized in 1874 by Major Walter Clopton Wingfield.

Grand Slam Tournaments

The four major Grand Slam tournaments—Wimbledon, the US Open, the French Open, and the Australian Open—are the most prestigious events in tennis. Each tournament has unique playing conditions, from the grass courts of Wimbledon to the clay courts of Roland Garros.

Legends of the Sport

Some of the greatest tennis players include Roger Federer, Rafael Nadal, and Novak Djokovic, who have dominated men's tennis in the 21st century. On the women's side, Serena Williams, Steffi Graf, and Martina Navratilova have set records that still stand today.

Strategies and Playing Styles

Different players have distinct playing styles, from powerful baseline hitters to serve-and-volley specialists. Tennis requires a blend of speed, precision, and mental toughness. Coaches and analysts study tactics such as deep defensive play, aggressive net approaches, and strong second serves.
